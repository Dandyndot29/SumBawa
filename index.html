Benefits of using husks for plants

To bind nutrients in plants

In general, rice husks are used in the process of loosening the soil to bind nutrients to plants. Each burnt husk has many large, but permanent, lightweight pores. The use of these pores is very good for binding and absorbing nutrients. when nutrients can be absorbed properly, then when watered they do not dissolve. Even so, the nutrient content in rice husks should be mixed using artificial fertilizers so that the nutrients will increase

Improving the level of acidity in the soil

now rice husk has been widely used as a planting medium for plants. The benefits of using roasted husks themselves can also improve the level of acidity in the soil. Meanwhile, good soil for growing growing media is neutral. Pumpkin husks have a relatively high pH value of around 8.5% to 9.0. Of course this is very suitable for use in acidic soils to increase soil pH. In addition, the silica content contained in roasted husks will strengthen the leaves, as a result the leaves become more erect and strengthen the plant.

Isolates disease and absorbs toxins in plants

In fact, pests and diseases in plants result in non-aporistic plant growth and development. In general, the cause of a plant having disease is caused by microorganisms in the form of viruses, bacteria and fungi. Diseased plants will experience problems in their growth and development. The gift of roasted husks can also isolate disease and absorb toxins in plants. Thus, roasted husks are useful as absorbents to suppress several pathogenic microbes.
